Summary

I'm a Distinguished Engineer at Google, working on the Privacy team, where I work on a wide range of projects centered around making our users both be and feel safe.

Over my time at Google, I've headed a variety of other projects, including:

Ultra-high-capacity search (2004-8): building search engines on fundamentally different principles than the ones you'll read about in CS textbooks, to scale to several more zeros worth of documents and QPS than traditional search engines can; this system remains the backbone of production Google search.

Planet-scale distributed storage (2008-2011): building a system which combines the properties of a storage system and a CDN which makes things like "the existence of large numbers of datacenters" and "the finiteness of the speed of light" implementation details to be hidden from the user. The resulting system specializes in eventually-consistent blob serving, and serves things such as Google's photos and the attachments for GMail.

Chief Architect of Social (2011-2015), which in addition to including general technical responsibility for system architecture also included being in charge of finding and fixing problems of all sorts, ranging from product to legal to PR. Key focus areas included user privacy and data protection, and solving the problem of harassment on the Internet.

The Google Assistant's infrastructure (2015-2016), where I was responsible for turning a bunch of disparate bits lying around the company into a unified system which can take on the challenge of developing true conversational AI, be a useful and thoughtful Assistant, and deploy on a wide variety of platforms.
